1. CAPITAL UTILIZATION RATE
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Production config:
  max_position_pct_of_equity = 15%
  max_concurrent_positions   = 8
  portfolio_max_leverage     = 2.0x

At every capital level ($100K to $1M+):
  Per-position max    = capital × 15%
  Max deployed        = 8 × per-position = 120% of capital
  Leverage capacity   = 200% of capital
  Utilization rate    = 120% / 200% = 60.0%
  Idle capacity       = 80% of capital (40% of leverage)

The 60% utilization is invariant to capital scale because both
max_deployed and leverage_capacity scale linearly with capital.


2. BINDING CONSTRAINT ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Constraint hierarchy (which constraint binds first):

  1st: MAX_CONCURRENT (8 positions × 15% = 120% of capital)
       This is the PRIMARY binding constraint at ALL capital levels.
       Caps total deployed notional at 120% of capital.

  2nd: MAX_LEVERAGE (2.0x = 200% of capital)
       NEVER binds under current config because max_concurrent
       prevents reaching 200% leverage.

  3rd: FACTOR LIMITS
       CHF (20%):         $20,000 for 5 assets → $4,000/asset
       AUD (25%):         $25,000 for 4 assets → $6,250/asset
       NZD (25%):         $25,000 for 4 assets → $6,250/asset
       JPY (25%):         $25,000 for 4 assets → $6,250/asset
       GOLD (15%):        $15,000 for 1 asset → $15,000/asset
       FX_MAJOR (40%):    $40,000 for 7 assets → $5,714/asset
       FX_CROSS (40%):    $40,000 for 18 assets → $2,222/asset
       US_EQUITY (25%):   $25,000 for 1 asset → $25,000/asset

  4th: DRAWDOWN TAPER
       Linear reduction from 100% → 50% as drawdown goes from -5% → -15%.
       Only activates during drawdown > 5%.

  5th: RISK PER TRADE (2% max risk per trade)
       Caps loss at 2% of capital per trade per day.

Result: 40% of leverage capacity is structurally inaccessible due to
the max_concurrent=8 cap. To achieve full 2.0x leverage, either:
  (a) Increase max_concurrent to 14 (14 × 15% = 210%, capped at 200%)
  (b) Increase max_position_pct to 25% at current 8 concurrent
  (c) Increase max_leverage to 2.5x to match current 8 × 15% = 120%
  (d) Some combination

4. WALK-FORWARD VS LIVE CONCURRENT POSITIONS — CRITICAL DISCREPANCY
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Walk-forward data (449 trading days, 22 assets):

  Mean concurrent:     18.3
  Median:              19
  Min:                  1
  Max:                 22
  P95:                 22
  P99:                 22
  Std:                  3.3

  Days with >8 concurrent positions: 435/449 = 96.9%
  Days with >14 concurrent positions: 422/449 = 94.0%

  Live cap:              8 (max_concurrent_positions)
  Gap from mean:      10.3 positions below walk-forward average
  Gap from median:    11 positions below walk-forward median
  Days unconstrained:  14/449 = 3.1%

  The backtest (walk-forward) assumes 18.3 concurrent positions on average.
  The live system caps at 8. This means:
  - The backtest's performance reflects ~18 concurrent positions
  - Live deployment gets at most 8 (44% of backtest's average)
  - 96.9% of trading days would hit the concurrent cap and be forced
    to reject signal entries that the backtest assumed would execute

  Implications for scaling linearity (R² = 1.0 in Phase 2):
  The Phase 2 scaling analysis assumes returns scale linearly with capital.
  This is ONLY valid if the number of concurrent positions scales with
  capital. With max_concurrent=8, it does NOT — total notional is capped
  at 120% regardless of capital. The analysis is misleading.

7. RECOMMENDATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
7.1 IMMEDIATE — Fix max_concurrent discrepancy
     The walk-forward data supports 14-18 concurrent positions.
     Raising max_concurrent from 8 to 14 would:
     - Increase utilization from 60% to 100% (full 2x leverage)
     - Match walk-forward assumptions (6% of days still constrained vs 97%)
     - Unlock ~$80K additional deployed capital at $100K

     Risk: 14 × 15% = 210% > 200% leverage cap → leverage becomes
     the binding constraint at 14 positions. Maximum useful concurrent
     positions = floor(2.0 / 0.15) = 13. Raise to 13-14.

7.2 MEDIUM — Adjust factor limits to match portfolio composition
     CHF limit (20%) is binding at $4,000/asset with 5 CHF-exposed assets.
     Consider raising to 25-30% given CHF assets are structurally important
     (mean concurrent CHF = 4.4, approaching all 5 simultaneously often).
     GOLD (15%) is underutilized (mean concurrent = 0.9 for 1 asset).
     No change needed for GOLD.

7.3 MEDIUM — Align backtest concurrency with live constraints
     The walk-forward backtest assumes unlimited concurrent positions.
     It should be re-run with max_concurrent=13 constraint to produce
     realistic live projections. The Phase 2 scaling analysis shows
     R²=1.0 linearity but this is misleading — it assumes all signals
     execute regardless of concurrency, which is not true in production.

7.4 LOW — Diversify across more factor groups
     Factor groups that are underutilized (US_EQUITY: 1 asset at 25%
     limit) could accommodate additional uncorrelated assets. The
     constraint is asset availability, not capital.
