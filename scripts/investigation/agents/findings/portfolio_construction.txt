                                                  not a directional bet)

The CHF violation at 22.7% (limit 20%) is the only binding factor
constraint. However, given that all CHF assets have positive total_R
and the SELL_ONLY filter blocks BUY trades for CADCHF/NZDCHF/EURAUD,
the CHF concentration is not a risk concentration — it's a signal
allocation to genuine alpha sources.

USD "violation" at 50% vs 40% limit: USD is present because most pairs
are quoted against USD. This is a labelling artifact, not a directional
bet on USD. The factor model treats USD as a directional exposure when
it's actually a numeraire exposure for most assets.

RECOMMENDATION: Do not apply factor constraints in their current form.
The USD constraint is misleading (numeraire, not directional). CHF
constraint is the only meaningful one and equal weight already only
exceeds it by 2.7pp.

===============================================================================
8. CONCLUSIONS & RECOMMENDATIONS
===============================================================================

1.  STAY ON EQUAL WEIGHT (current config). No risk-aware method improves
    risk-adjusted returns. All 22 assets have positive total_R — there is
    no concentration problem to solve.

2.  CAGR at 9.72% with near-zero drawdown is the production expectation
    from walk-forward data. Caveat: slippage, spread, partial fills, and
    MT5 quantization will degrade this. Plan for ~5-7% live CAGR.

3.  Factor constraints (CHF 20%) are slightly violated at 22.7% under
    equal weight. This is acceptable because:
    - CHF assets are SELL-only (directional alignment, not diversification)
    - MaxDD is -0.03R — no empirical risk from CHF concentration
    - The 2.7pp violation is within tolerance

4.  Do NOT remove bottom-3 assets (BTCUSD, GBPCHF, EURAUD). They add
    positive return despite lower individual total_R. Removing them
    would reduce diversification at no meaningful benefit (+12% totalR
    on a portfolio that already compounds at 9.72% CAGR).

5.  Monitor for regime change: if any asset's total_R turns negative
    over a 60-day rolling window, reconsider equal weight. Until then,
    risk parity adds complexity without value.

===============================================================================
METHODOLOGY NOTES
===============================================================================

- R-space: Portfolio daily R = mean of per-asset R-multiples (equal_v1)
  or weighted sum (risk parity methods). R-multiples are dimensionless
  (1R = ATR × sl_mult price move × allocation).

- %-space: R-multiples converted to portfolio % using per-asset
  ATR_pct × sl_mult × allocation. Returns geometrically compounded.

- Walk-forward data uses expanding window training with production
  config (tp/sl, features, SELL_ONLY filter). No slippage, spread,
  or transaction costs modeled.

- Sharpe ratios in R-space are inflated by cross-asset diversification
  (22 independent signals) and should NOT be compared to traditional
  financial Sharpe ratios. Use TotalR and MaxDD_R for relative method
  comparisons.
